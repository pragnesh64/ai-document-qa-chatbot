Animals are multicellular organisms that belong to the kingdom Animalia. They are characterized by their ability to move, consume organic material, and respond to their environment.

Classification of animals:
- Mammals: Warm-blooded vertebrates with hair or fur
- Birds: Feathered, winged, egg-laying vertebrates
- Reptiles: Cold-blooded vertebrates with scales
- Amphibians: Animals that live both in water and on land
- Fish: Aquatic vertebrates with gills and fins
- Insects: Six-legged arthropods with segmented bodies

Famous animals include:
- Lions: Known as the king of the jungle
- Elephants: Largest land mammals with excellent memory
- Dolphins: Highly intelligent marine mammals
- Eagles: Powerful birds of prey with excellent vision
- Pandas: Endangered bears native to China

Animals play crucial roles in ecosystems as predators, prey, pollinators, and decomposers.
